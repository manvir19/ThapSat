Payload

The Mid wave Multi-Band IR camera monitors greenhouse gases such as Carbon Monoxide, Carbon Dioxide, Methane, Nitrous Oxide, and Water vapour in the atmosphere through the 8 different spectral band filters. It comprises of Mid-Wave InfraRed detector, Imaging lens, Infrared filters and Optical fork sensor. The waveband range of this MWIR detector is 2-5Î¼m. The payload of the satellite works on the principle of absorption of light. Solar radiation is absorbed by different gaseous particles in a specific wavelength range based on their absorption spectra.

 

Calibration

We will have an experimental test setup for the calibration. This will provide us with knowledge about the absorption spectrum of the gases. For the calibration, we have proposed a test setup comprising a broadband IR light source, Bandpass filters, Neutral density filters, chopper, Thermal Power Sensor, Photodetector, gas chamber and DSO. The idea is to fill the gas cell with pollutant gases and the light is passed from one side of the gas cell and the output is taken from the other side of the cell. To increase light's path length, we are using mirrors as reflectors. The absorbed light reaches the detector which is connected to DSO. The absorption of light can be estimated by calculating the difference between the intensity of light when there is no gas present in the cell and when gases are present in the cell.
